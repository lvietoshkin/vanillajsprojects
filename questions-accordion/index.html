Why do octopuses have three hearts?

Two hearts pump blood to the gills, while the third sends it to the rest of the body. When they swim, the main heart actually stops, which is why octopuses prefer crawling over swimming. It’s also part of why they tire out quickly in open water.

Why do bananas glow under UV light?

As bananas ripen, their chlorophyll breaks down and creates compounds that fluoresce under ultraviolet light. To the human eye, they look normal—but under UV, they glow blue. Some scientists think this might signal ripeness to animals that can see UV light.

What’s the coldest place ever recorded on Earth?

The lowest temperature ever measured was −89.2°C (−128.6°F) at Antarctica’s Vostok Station. At that extreme, exposed skin can freeze in minutes. Scientists working there have to take extreme precautions just to step outside.
